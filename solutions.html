    • Innovative Cleanup Initiatives

    The Ocean Cleanup is a leading project deploying autonomous systems to collect plastics from the GPGP. Their technology targets floating debris using long floating barriers that concentrate the plastic for easier removal. They also focus on intercepting plastics in rivers before they reach the ocean.

    Recycling and Awareness Projects

    Organizations like Bracenet and 4ocean recycle recovered materials into products that fund further cleanup. These groups raise awareness, educate communities, and drive consumer action through transparency and engagement.

    Policy and Global Cooperation

    Governments and international organizations are taking steps by banning certain single-use plastics and improving waste management. Treaties like the UN’s Global Plastic Treaty aim to reduce plastic pollution at the source through cooperation and legislation.

    What You Can Do

    Reduce single-use plastics: say no to straws, bags, and plastic cutlery.
    Choose reusable alternatives: stainless steel bottles, cloth bags, and glass containers.
    Support legislation: vote for and advocate for plastic bans and improved recycling laws.
    Get involved: join beach cleanups, educate your community, and support eco-friendly brands.

    Every action counts. By changing our habits and supporting larger efforts, we can help protect the oceans for future generations.
