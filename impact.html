    • Threat to Marine Life

    Plastic pollution from the GPGP poses a major threat to marine animals. Sea turtles often mistake plastic bags for jellyfish, ingesting them with fatal consequences. Seabirds, like albatrosses, have been found feeding plastic fragments to their chicks, which can lead to internal injuries, starvation, and death.

    Ghost Nets and Entanglement

    Discarded fishing gear, known as ghost nets, represent a serious hazard. These nets drift through the ocean, entangling fish, dolphins, sea lions, and even whales. The entrapment can cause injury, suffocation, or drowning, significantly affecting marine biodiversity.

    Microplastics and the Food Chain

    As plastic degrades, it turns into microplastics that are ingested by plankton. These tiny organisms are at the base of the marine food web, meaning the plastic moves up the food chain—eventually reaching humans. Studies have shown microplastics in seafood, drinking water, and even human blood.

    Long-Term Consequences

    The environmental impact is far-reaching. Microplastics alter reproductive systems in aquatic animals, introduce toxic chemicals into ecosystems, and disrupt natural habitats. The long-term effects on human health and marine ecosystems are still being studied but are expected to be profound.
